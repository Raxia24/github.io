J. Robert Oppenheimer

"The father of the atomic bomb"

Here's a time line of J. Robert Oppenheimer life

  • 1904 - Born in New York City, New York
  • 1922 - Enters Harvard University at the age of 18
  • 1925 - Graduates summa cum laude from Harvard with a degree in chemistry
  • 1926 - Travels to Europe to study at the University of Cambridge under physicist J.J. Thomson
  • 1927 - Moves to the University of Göttingen to study theoretical physics under Max Born
  • 1929 - Receives his Ph.D. in theoretical physics from the University of Göttingen
  • 1931 - Returns to the United States and becomes a professor at the University of California, Berkeley
  • 1939 - Writes a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning about the potential of Nazi Germany developing nuclear weapons, which eventually leads to the establishment of the Manhattan Project
  • 1942 - Appointed as the scientific director of the Manhattan Project, leading the effort to develop the atomic bomb
  • 1945 - Witnesses the first successful test of an atomic bomb in Alamogordo, New Mexico
  • 1946 - Oppenheimer is awarded the Medal for Merit for his role in the Manhattan Project
  • 1947 - Becomes the director of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey
  • 1954 - Faces a security hearing and loses his security clearance due to his past associations with communist sympathizers
  • 1963 - Receives the Enrico Fermi Award from President Lyndon B. Johnson for his contributions to science
  • 1967 - Dies in Princeton, New Jersey, at the age of 62

Now I am become death, the destroyer of worlds.

-- J. Robert Oppenheimer
